What is Investing?

Investing means allocating money with the expectation of generating profit or income. Unlike saving, investing involves calculated risks to grow your wealth over time.

Why Invest?

  • Beat inflation: Cash loses value (3-7% annually in Kenya)
  • Wealth building: Compound growth over time
  • Passive income: Dividends/rental income

Main Ways to Invest

1. Stocks (Equities)

Buying shares of companies like Safaricom, KCB

Example: 100 Safaricom shares @ KSh 20 = KSh 2,000 investment

2. Real Estate

Physical properties or REITs

Example: Buying a rental apartment in Nairobi

3. Bonds

Loaning to governments/companies

Example: Kenya Eurobond paying 7% annually

Expected Returns Comparison:
  • Stocks: 10-15% annually
  • Real Estate: 8-12% annually + appreciation
  • Bonds: 7-12% annually
  • Savings Account: 4% annually (often below inflation)

Stock Trading Basics

Stocks represent ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a shareholder.

Example: If you buy Safaricom (NSE: SCOM) at KSh 20 per share and sell at KSh 30:
  • Profit per share = KSh 10

Key Stock Market Concepts

Market Orders vs. Limit Orders

Market order: Buy/sell immediately at current price

Limit order: Buy/sell only at specified price or better

Bid-Ask Spread

The difference between the highest price a buyer will pay (bid) and the lowest price a seller will accept (ask)

Dividends

Company profits distributed to shareholders (e.g., Safaricom pays ~KSh 1.10 per share annually)

Forex Trading Fundamentals

Complete guide to currency trading and macroeconomic factors.

The main aim in Forex Trading is to buy cheap and sell expensive


Example if EURUSD is trading at 100 and you think it will raise in value or rise from 100 to example 120 you buy at 100 and set your Take profit(TP) at 120

If you think the price will go down you sell at 100 and set your Take profit(TP) at 80

In forex trading currencies are quoted in pairs


Example EUR/USD is the Euro against the US Dollar

When you buy EUR/USD you are buying the Euro and selling the US Dollar

When you sell EUR/USD you are selling the Euro and buying the US Dollar

In forex trading the first currency is called the base currency and the second currency is called the quote currency


Example EUR/USD the Euro is the base currency and the US Dollar is the quote currency

If EUR/USD is trading at 1.1000 it means 1 Euro is equal to 1.1000 US Dollars

In forex trading if the price of a currency pair goes up it means the base currency is strengthening against the quote currency


Example if EUR/USD goes from 1.1000 to 1.2000 it means the Euro is strengthening against the US Dollar

If EUR/USD goes from 1.1000 to 1.0000 it means the Euro is weakening against the US Dollar

In forex trading if the price of a currency pair goes down it means the base currency is weakening against the quote currency


Example if EUR/USD goes from 1.2000 to 1.1000 it means the Euro is weakening against the US Dollar

If EUR/USD goes from 1.0000 to 1.1000 it means the Euro is strengthening against the US Dollar

HOW DO YOU KNOW IF PRICE WILL GO UP OR DOWN...?

You need to analyze the market using technical analysis,fundamental analysis or sentiment analysis

Technical analysis is the study of past price action to predict future price action

Fundamental analysis is the study of economic and political factors that affect the value

Sentiment analysis is the study of how traders are feeling about the market

Key Forex Concepts

Pips

Smallest price move (0.0001 for most pairs)

Example: EUR/USD moves from 1.1000 to 1.1001 = 1 pip

Leverage

Borrowed capital to increase position size (e.g., 1:100 leverage means $1 controls $100)

Spread

Difference between buy and sell prices (broker's commission)

Volume Concepts

Bid/Ask Volume

Shows buying (at ask) vs. selling (at bid) pressure

Delta

Difference between buying and selling volume

Positive delta: More buying pressure

VWAP

Volume Weighted Average Price - benchmark for institutional trading

Volume Spikes

Unusual trading activity often precedes big moves

Python for Financial Analysis

Example using yfinance to analyze stocks:

import yfinance as yf
import pandas as pd

# Create a Ticker object for Apple (AAPL)
aapl = yf.Ticker("AAPL")

# Get financial statements
income_stmt = aapl.income_stmt
balance_sheet = aapl.balance_sheet
cashflow = aapl.cashflow

# Get historical data
historical_data = aapl.history(period="ytd")

# Analyze key metrics

print(f"AAPL P/E: {pe_ratio}, Market Cap: ${market_cap:,}")
